Paris, France

Amour Amour

Hitting Paris for 48 hours is the Dream, I also had the added benefit of having a sister living in Paris, so finding my way around and getting the low down on where to hang, what to see and what to avoid saved me a shit load of time!

I splashed out and stayed in the Hotel Central Saint Germain located @ 3 rue Champollion, 75005 and by splashing out I mean heavily discounted rate due to the renovation work being carried out in the hotel, look out for these deals, great way of staying in nice hotels at a fraction of the cost, this is the third time I have done this and iv still yet to be disturbed by the construction work, plus your on a city break so get the fuck out of the hotel.

The Latin Quarter Stay here! Why - Activity, cafes, bars, restaurants, central, young, vibrant etc.

There is just so much to see and do in Paris, jump on the red city tour buses, I honestly feel they give you a good overall history and view point. If you do hit these tours make sure you can jump on and off, you don't want to be a spectator, get off the bus touch the monuments, bump into locals, take pictures, chill and take in the moment.

I really feel that most people don't take in the moment. Find a spot, whether its looking down the halls of Notre Dame, are gazing across the Seine, you are in Paris take it in feel the vibe and be happy that you are seeing and experiencing somewhere different. We have all seen those tourists who are just snap happy, don't be one of those, there is more to the travel experience then filling up an SD card.

I had three manin objectives. 1) Eiffel Tower, 2) Notre Dame de Paris, and 3) Arc de Triumph.

Eiffel Tower

The queue is always massive and can eat up way too much time, id suggest taking the stairs it’s only 704 steps and a nice way to get the blood flowing, and work off the pain au chocolate.

Notre Dame de Paris

Its beautiful and worth a visit, interesting history. Also one of the many love lock bridges is close by… if thats you're thing.

Arc de Triumph

Enjoy the walk down the champs elysee, hit a few shops till you reach one of the most iconic sights in Paris. You can go up the Arc, but the view has nothing on the Eiffel.

Cafe

You would be surprised how difficult it is to find a cool coffee place in Paris, a place where you can chill with good music, buzzing atmosphere, wifi and something to eat. Check out 10 belles (open till 6pm) @ 10 Rue de la Grange aux Belles.

10 Belles

Dinner

French cusine the best in the world... id argue with that. Try L'Agence if you want something 'traditionally' French. It goes without saying, but the wines are awsome.. @ 36 rue des Vinaigriers.

Hit up Pigalle area, nice bars and clubs also you will get to see the showgirls that have made the area famous (Moulin Rouge).

Moulin Rouge definatly worth a visit.

Night

Paris at night is an expensive motherfucker, hit Zelda @ 6 Ru Bichat , Dirty Dick @ 10 Rue Crochet , also Hotel Amour @ 8 Rue de Navarin. Its a good place to socialise and the young Parisians love any opportunity to practice their english so starting conversations is easy. If you want to fit in even more buy an e-cigarette every second person seems to be sucking on them in Paris at the moment :)

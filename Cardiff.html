Cardiff, Wales

6 leprechauns and RWC

Nothing like a rugby trip with the lads, and no better place than Cardiff, a city built around rugby and packed to the gills with Irish fans.

I've been to Cardiff a few times over the years for Rugby matches, and absolutely love the place, the locals are great, even ordering a taxi the operator tells us he is 'Green today, Red every other day'. Finding accommodation when Millennium Stadium is packing in 75,000 fans can make Cardiff shit expensive as there is a limited amount of hotels. We camped in Llandaff Rugby club, which is a 3km walk to the city centre. If you happen to have a massive bank account during a rugby match you should stay in the Hilton or Novotel Cardiff Centre. Both are central and really affordable when there isn't a rugby match on in Millennium Stadium.

St Mary Street - Main street for shopping, bars, clubs etc.

Cardiff is super small and great craic when a rugby match is on, as the whole city is dominated by the fans. Also the bars and clubs are open till late and there is a good amount of things to do during the day.

We had three main objectives. 1) IRE v FRA RWC Millennium Stadium, 2) Hit FanZone, and 3) Cardiff Castle .

IRE v FRA RWC 2015

No better way to spend the day than, watching your country play and win at Millennium Stadium in the group stage of the Rugby World Cup. The atmosphere for this match was amazing, the Irish fans came out in force and drowned out any hope of a French victory! When the roof of the stadium is closed it's an even more impressive sight.

FanZone

The day before the match and the day of the match we hit up the FanZone which is located in Cardiff Arms Park (beside Millennium Stadium). Great way to get pumped before a match with live music, big screens showing other games, and best of all its free entry!

Cardiff Castle

A Victorian Gothic castle built on the remains of Norman and Roman ruins. Had to visit this before we left Cardiff, as it was important for us to see a little of the city. It is also the most popular historical attraction in the city . The castle is beautiful and only costs Â£13 for a guided tour which lasts 50min.

Cafe/lunch

Shit ton of coffee shops around Cardiff, we chilled out in Uncommon Ground Coffee, very hipster but has really nice coffee and chocolate brownies, which we of course smashed into our mouths, located in the Royal Arcade. Also check out the best coffee in Cardiff as voted by the locals Pettigrew Tea Rooms.

Uncommon Ground Coffee @ 10-12 Royal Arcade, Cardiff CF10 1AE

Tip

Cardiff is all about 'pre-booking' you need to pre-book your taxi or you ain't getting home after your night out, you will need to book lunch spots and obviously a place for dinner or you ain't eating. .

Dinner

As usual, burger joints ruled our menu for the weekend, but what do you expect when Cardiff has a hidden gem down the Old Brewery Quarter, the one and only Five Guys which has the best fucking burgers and cajan chips on the planet! We also ate in Greenwood & Brown before the match (pre-booked), this place was amazing, literally the best fried calamari I have ever had! Also within 8 seconds of Millennium Stadium so perfect for a pre match feast.

Five Guys @ The Old Brewery Quarter, St Mary's Street, Cardiff CF10 1AD

Night

Loads of bars and clubs in Cardiff and strangely enough loads of strip clubs... Want somewhere mental, with a very mixed crowed hit Walkabout, open late and can get messy. Want a traditional pub with live music hit Dempseys on Castle Street. Lab 22 is an upmarket cocktail bar, creative cocktails with a bit of theatre .

Belfast, Northern Ireland

Titanic... Tayto sandwich

This was super last minute, as I didn't fancy another weekend doing shit all! Emailed three friends at 11am on a Friday, sorted out a car and accommodation by lunch, hit the road at 5.43 p.m after work and in Belfast for the weekend... beautiful.

When I want a city break in Ireland other than Dublin, Belfast is top of the list, I've had so many great memories in Belfast and there is always a ton of stuff to do, and this last minute trip was no different.

Belfast is small, lots to see and more history than you can shake a stick at. We stayed in Belfast International Hostel, which is a 1 km walk from the city centre, not a bad location at all, clubs and bars just around the corner and cheap as chips!

Victoria Square - Shopping area

Belfast can be conquered on a budget, you will see loads, relax, and have a great time! Natural beauty and man made marvels to be embraced. Its got it all.

We had three main objectives. 1) Titanic Exhibition, 2) Giant's Causeway, and 3) The Color Run .

Titanic Exhibition

A monument to Belfast's maritime heritage on the site of the former Harland & Wolff shipyard in the city's Titanic Quarter. Costs around £15, nice way to spend 2 hours, if it's lashing rain and you fancy learning about why Rose didn't let Jack onto the door.

Giant's Causeway

Made up of 40,000 interlocking basalt columns, the result of an ancient volcanic eruption. Surprisingly small, thought it would be a huge area. It's a beautiful place, we went really early so that it would be quiet, and not thronged with tourists which was key. If you go during peak times it resembles a playground with people climbing the columns, so avoid that shit for a real experience.

Color Run

Joined this run last minute, one of my friends was MCing and was killing it on stage! Running around the Titanic Quarter getting powdered paint fucked in your face was surprisingly good fun and not a bad way to sweat out the pints and shit take away food from the previous night . Obviously The Color Run is a once off event so for an alternative activity, I suggest hiking Black Mountain, which looks over Belfast, takes about an hour to walk, and the views are sweet. Start the hike on Divis Road.

Cafe/lunch

Loads of coffee shops around Belfast, we hunted out 'the wee cafe' which was a pop up, for some Tayto crisp sandwiches, which were lovely and only cost £4. For some really nice brunch and coffee hit Established, located in the Belfast's Cathedral Quarter.

Established @ 54 Hill St, Belfast BT1 2LB

Bonus

Be smart and get around the city on the Belfast Bikes. You can rent bikes for £5 for three days and cycle around and do your own tour with a tourist bike map or you can pay for a "Black Taxi Cab" guided tour which does all the historical sites for £25 for two people.

Dinner

No real surprise but we ate in casual burger joints and bbq restaurants, quick and easy especially considering we were on the go non stop. For a really nice burger hit City Picnic which has a great atmosphere and really really good burgers. Also treat yourself and grab a shake loaded with your favourite sweets, I love being a fat shit when on holidays.

City Picnic @ 6 Castle St, Belfast BT1 1HB

Night

No shortage of great bars and clubs in Belfast. Check out Kelly's Cellars for a casual place to hang, with traditional Irish music. Want cocktails and a large Champagne menu hit Café Vaudeville for a Parisian/Baz Luhrman style evening. The Hudson Bar is a hipster bar set over three floors, with a large craft beer menu and they even brew their own beer. If you're a boring prick there are loads of Wetherspoons around the place.

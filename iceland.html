Reykjavik, Iceland

Ice and Fire

Iceland is spectacular, a photographers paradise! I went with my girlfriend for 3 days, we were so pumped to be in Iceland, we had been talking about it for months and had planned everything to military precision.

First thing that needs to be said is that Iceland is an expensive mother fucker, like all Nordic countries, but we found it more expensive than Sweden and Norway. The Icelanders are so nice, and really friendly, without a shadow of doubt you will end up chatting to a few locals over a beer any night you are in Reykjavik.

Reykjavik is small and you'll have it done in a day which is great because it gives you more time to explore the amazing landscape. We stayed in Reykjavik Lights Hotel, which is a 3km walk from the city center, not a bad location and the walk is grand as long as you bring your Arctic gear with you!

Sólheimasandur black sand beach

Regardless of the time of year you will find a huge amount of things to do. Depending on budget you can do everything from diving in the ice lagoons, quad biking across lava sand beaches, whale watching or a casual flight over an active volcano.

We had three main objectives. 1) Explore Reykjavik, 2) Golden Circle, and 3) Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon .

Reykjavik

Only an hour off the plane already exploring Reykjavik, want a nice view of the city hit the Hallgrímskirkja (Reykjavik Church, above picture). The opera house is also worth a visit and just a short walk down you can see the whaling and whale watching ships sit side by side... if you are inclined you can grab a whale kebab in the local fish shops.

Golden Circle

South Iceland, covering roughly 300 km looping from Reykjavík into central Iceland and back. Comprises Thingvellir National Park Rift Valley, Gullfoss Waterfall, Strokkur geyser and hot spring area. Takes about a day in total, either rent a car or jump on a bus tour, I'd recommend renting a car, as it's probably the cheapest and most fun way to explore South Iceland.

Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon

First off its 400km from Reykjavik but saying that the whole drive is breathtaking, you will come across huge mountain ranges, lava fields (which looks like telly tubby fields, or mounds of giant peas) and vast spans of nothingness but black sand beaches. If you plan this well you can hit up loads of stuff along the way such as Selfoss (amazing waterfall), Vik (small town and black sand beach) , Skaftafell (glacier)

Cafe/lunch

Packed lunches are your friend when in Iceland, as most of the time you will be in the middle of nowhere. If you want a nice breakfast or brunch at a good price, hit The Laundromat Cafe, really nice breakfasts and juices! You can also wash the volcano dust off your gear in the fully functioning laundry room in the basment of the café.

The Laundromat Cafe @ Austurstræti 9, 101 Reykjavík,

Bonus

The other obvious trip is to the Blue Lagoon. It is beside the airport which is about 50 km from Reykjavik, so the done thing is to visit it either on the way to or from the airport , they have places to leave your luggage, so no need to stress. Its nice and relaxing but the smell of sulphur is so bad...

Dinner

We basically ate in casual burger joints and had Dominos Pizza one night, reason being we spent so much time traveling and exploring. Usually finding a nice places to eat would be top of my list, but the beauty of the landscape consumed everything we wanted to do and see. In Reykjavik, hitting a good restraunt requires you to book. Check out Apotek which has a great atmsphere, Fridrik V and Kopar in the port. Or if you want a crap burger check out the Chuck Noris Grill on the main street :)

Apotek @ Austurstræti 16, Reykjavík,

Night

Reykjavik has a tiny population, roughly 120,000 so finidng nightclubs and buzzing bars can be difficult at times. Good few places along the main street to check out. Most bars close by 1 a.m. from Sunday-Thursday and stay open until as late as 4.30 a.m. on Friday and Saturday nights. The legal drinking age in Iceland is 20, but many bars impose their own age limit of 21 or 22. Check out Ölstofa Kormáks og Skjaldar for a casual place to hang, if cocktails are your thing then Slippbarinn is the place for you. Our favourite place was Harlem it's a little hipster but due to its small size it feels busy even if it's just you sitting at the bar.

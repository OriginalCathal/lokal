Edinburgh, Scotland

As the Scots say...

Edinburgh for a long weekend, tours, museums, whiskey, haggis and loads of fucking bagpipes!

Traveling with your parents has its benefits, one being getting hooked up with a sweet hotel, right in the centre of all the action! We stayed in the Radisson Blu, located on The Royal Mile. Shit tone of cheaper options located in the Grassmarket / Cowgate area, such as Budget Backpackers... actually Argyle Backpackers is a great hostel in two Victorian buildings. Both are close to Edinburgh’s buzzing centre.

The Golden Mile

Edinburgh is so fucking cool, there is loads to do the people are sound, and if you are in any way a fan of Harry Potter you will see JKs inspiration, the Medieval architecture is simply amazing.

As per usual I set down, three main things I wanted to do in Edinburgh, with plenty of room to add other shit in and go with the flow.

So the three main objectives. 1) Visit Edinburgh Castle, 2) Hit National Museum of Scotland and 3) Drink and Learn about Whisky till I'm pickled .

Edinburgh Castle

This Castle dominates the skyline, exploring the castle and taking in the history is amazing. Try get to the castle befor 1pm as they fire a cannon everyday to mark mid-day, and dont forget to see the Crown Jewels they are very impressive . Located @ Castle Rock, Edinburgh.

National Museum OF Scotland

When looking to escape the pissing rain hit the Museum... and see Dolly the sheep, the first successful clone of a mammal from an adult cell, or the Ancient Egyptian exhibitions, or one of Elton John's extravagant suits... its a pretty crazy and interesting Museum to say the least. Located @ Chambers St, Edinburgh.

Whisky Experiance

So many places to do whisky tasting, its great fun with a group and you will learn a load about whisky. Even if you are not a whisky drinker you have to do this in Edinburgh its a right of passage, and you will get locked. Located @ 354 Castlehill, Edinburgh .

Cafe

Edinburgh is a city with loads of steps and hills, so it can be nice to take it easy, sit back and relax. Myself and my mum after sneeking onto a walking tour decided we needed to chill and indulge on chocolate biscut cake and strong coffee. We ate cake and coffee in Mimi's Picnic Parlor Located @ 253 The Royal Mile. Also check out the roof top cafe/restaurant in harvey nichols called The Forth Floor Bar, really nice and savage city views.

Dinner

This trip I ate like a king, Edinburgh is a nice little mecca for foodies, we ate in not one but two Michelin star restaurants on this trip... the first being Ondine the second the Kitchen, and they where both fucking unreal. The reason I liked them both so much is not just because of the food, it was the atmosphere, they where buzzing and filled with young people out enjoying themselves! The Kitchin was located @ 78 Commercial Quay, Edinburgh. Ondine was located @ 2 George IV Bridge.

Ondine

At night and there’s nowhere in the city more lively and bustling than the Old Town, crowds in the Grassmarket spill out of the rows of bars and create a great atmosphere in the street. If you head towards the Cowgate, or walk up the cobbled streets towards the Royal Mile, you’ll find clubs like Cabaret Voltaire, Electric Circus, Bow Bar the list is endless.

That's a wrap

Edinburgh is such a great city to visit, its filled with history, culture and a youthful population. The place is buzzing and will leave you wanting more. I had a great time with my family, visitng the museums, hitting the best restaurants, sampling the whisky and generaly having a fun filled weekend... Also forgot to add if traveling from Ireland you will be on a really small proppeler plane that is bumpy as shit. Enjoy.

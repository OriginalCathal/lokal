Faro, Portugal

Obrigado

Portugal is generally a place where families spend two weeks for the annual summer holidays for me I had 72 hours, flew in after work on Friday and back in the office Monday morning, tan topped up, and feeling fresh as a daisy.

The Algarve is a European holiday mecca, so finding accommodation to suit any budget is shit easy, further north it's a little more package holiday friendly, but down south closer to Faro you are looking at high end hotels and massive villas, Quinta Do Lago has two bloody Four Seasons hotels on the same 2 mile stretch of road. For my flying visit, I stayed in a lovely villa located between Quinta Do Lago and Val do Lobo, golfers paradise.

Waves beach bar, to watch the sun go down

Portugal is amazing, the coast line is incredible, the people are so nice and unlike Marbella, it has a sense of still being untouched, you won't find high rises up and down the coast it has a natural beauty. Exploring hidden beaches and islands is so much better than being packed on a busy beach.

I really look forward to visiting Portugal as the food is amazing, and depending on which Portuguese restaurant you hit, you can get amazing dishes with influences from the Portuguese colonies.

I had three main objectives. 1) Paddle Board, 2) Faro Island, and 3) Visit Old Town Faro .

Paddly Boarding

Only 2 hours off the plane and straigh to Quinta beach for some morning SUPing with my cousin was the dream! €20 for 2 hours just cant beat it. Watersports on every beach, hit the lake at Ria Formosa Natural Park for a quite watersports center.

Faro Island

This is the quietest most tropical beach you will find in the Algarve, cost €10 by boat to get too, takes 20 min from Faro old town and is just unreal, pack up your shit for the day and chill on this untouched natural island!

Old Town Faro

The Algarve is littered with modern shopping centers and tourist areas with bars and restraunts, for a change go to Faro old town and experiance the fish markets and explore the Fort. Also check out the 'skull chapel' human remains plastered into the walls... so weird

Cafe/lunch

Finding a nice place to eat in the Algarve is shit easy, Hit Izzys Beach Restaurant after a day on the water, great food and live music. Probably best to make a booking, Gralic Prawns for starter and the Portuguese steak for main course and your laughing.

Izzys Beach Restaurant

Dinner

Where do I start! So many amazing places to eat in the Algarve, but by far my two favourite places have to be Parrilla Natural and Gormet Natural both owned by the same team, serving mouth watering Uruguayan steaks, (ask for a table outide in either restaurant). Also you can't go wrong with 'A Quinta' or 'Birds Nest Restaurant' eat in any of those four place and you'll be fucking delighted with yourself.

Parrilla Natural

Night

Such a seasonal place so unless your here during the busy summer months or the Easter break, any other time is like being in an old folks home. Beach bars are the place to hang, hit up Izzys for some sun downers, or Julias.
